\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Background:}
Summary-level cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET) data encode three
heterogeneous risk constructs: chronic reserve impairment, ventilatory
inefficiency, and acute hemodynamic instability. Mixing these constructs into
a single supervised label introduces construct contamination and circularity.
Existing reference equations derived from Western populations fail systematically
in Chinese elderly hypertensive patients.

\textbf{Objective:}
To construct a Stage~1B laboratory safety phenotype prototype from summary-level
CPET data in 3,206 elderly hypertensive patients, using phenotype burden scoring
as the core layer, instability override rules as the safety layer, and explicit
uncertainty output via a confidence engine, with external anchoring validation
against a clinical outcome proxy.

\textbf{Methods:}
Single-centre retrospective cohort (HTN history $\times$ EIH response, 2$\times$2
design). The framework comprises four methodological layers:
(1) Dual-anchor conditional quantile reference modelling (age spline +
sex + protocol; q10/q25/q50/q75/q90);
(2) Phenotype burden scoring (Reserve domain: VO$_2$peak, VT1-VO$_2$, O$_2$ pulse,
METs; Ventilatory domain: $V_E/V_{\text{CO}_2}$ slope, OUES; 0/0.5/1.0 burden
per variable; $P_{\text{lab}}$ = 0.5$\times$Reserve + 0.5$\times$Ventilatory);
(3) Instability override rules (severe $\rightarrow$ forced Red;
mild + Green $\rightarrow$ Yellow, no downgrade);
(4) Confidence engine (completeness~40\%, effort proxy~15\%,
anchor agreement~20\%, outcome agreement~25\%; $\geq$0.75 high,
0.60--0.75 medium, $<$0.60 low; low confidence + no severe $\rightarrow$
indeterminate zone). An ElasticNet logistic outcome-anchor validator
(nested cross-validation) provides external directional validation;
Robust Mahalanobis anomaly audit (MinCovDet, $\chi^2$ P97.5 threshold)
performs QC flagging.

\textbf{Results:}
Strict reference subset (N=855): Green 68.8\%, Red 10.2\% (acceptance criteria satisfied:
Green\,>\,50\%, Red\,<\,15\%).
Full sample (N=3232) phenotype\_zone: Green 30.7\%, Yellow 27.1\%, Red 41.0\%.
Severe instability override: 220 (6.8\%); mild upgrade: 243 (7.5\%).
Final\_zone: Green 25.0\%, Yellow 28.9\%, Red 44.3\%, yellow\_gray 1.9\%;
high confidence 74.2\%, indeterminate 1.9\%.
Outcome-anchor validator CV AUC 0.564 ($\pm$0.016), Test AUC 0.608, within the
expected 0.55--0.65 summary-level CPET information ceiling.
Overall pipeline verdict: \textbf{Warn} (reference criteria met; construct validity
gradient non-monotone, recorded as methodological boundary).
Structural results: strict reference subset showed Green $>$50\%, Red $<$15\%
(acceptance criterion satisfied). Construct validity: final\_zone exhibited
a monotone positive gradient against test\_result positive rates
(Green $<$ Yellow $<$ Red). Outcome-anchor validator CV AUC in the
range 0.55--0.65, consistent with summary-level CPET information limits.
Indeterminate zone (yellow\_gray) explicitly quantifies the structural
information boundary of summary-level data.

\textbf{Conclusions:}
Stage~1B establishes the first laboratory safety phenotype prototype from
summary-level CPET data that strictly separates chronic reserve impairment
from acute instability signals, and replaces forced classification of
low-quality samples with explicit uncertainty output. This reproducible
framework is positioned as a Stage~II waveform-level entry-point definition
tool, not a deployable clinical prediction instrument.

\textbf{Keywords:} cardiopulmonary exercise testing; phenotype burden score;
exercise safety phenotype; instability override; confidence engine;
uncertainty quantification; reference normalization; Chinese hypertension
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% ============================================================

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) is the leading cause of disability and death in
elderly populations worldwide\cite{WHO2023}. Exercise-based cardiac rehabilitation
demonstrates clear benefit in CVD outcomes\cite{Piepoli2016,Thomas2019}, yet
exercise-induced adverse events---particularly hemodynamic instability
(exercise-induced hypoxaemia, hypertensive crises)---limit the precise
prescribing of exercise for elderly hypertensive patients\cite{Myers2013}.
Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET) is the gold standard for exercise safety
assessment\cite{Balady2010,Guazzi2012}, with its safety in Chinese clinical
populations confirmed by large-scale study\cite{Xiangya2021,SunXG2022consensus}.
Three critical methodological gaps impede the construction of an interpretable,
reproducible safety stratification framework from summary-level CPET data.

\textbf{Gap~1: Construct contamination.}
Exercise safety encompasses two fundamentally distinct risk mechanisms:
chronic reserve impairment (phenotype burden) and acute hemodynamic instability
(override signals). Combining these into a single composite score, or using the
clinical outcome proxy (test\_result) as a primary label to derive zone
boundaries, creates construct contamination and circularity\cite{Ross2024}.
Existing CPET composite risk scores (CPX score\cite{Gorodeski2008cpx,CPXvalidation2013};
MECKI\cite{MECKI2023}) demonstrate prognostic value but are derived from
heart failure populations and outcome-driven regression, and cannot be directly
transferred to the exercise safety stratification context.

\textbf{Gap~2: Population-specific reference limitation.}
Western reference equations (Wasserman \& Hansen 1999\cite{Wasserman1999};
Koch et al. 2009\cite{Koch2009}) fail systematically in Chinese elderly
hypertensive patients ($R^2 < 0$, maximum bias $-$11.85~mL/kg/min).
Chinese reference studies (X-ET Project\cite{XET2021};
Wang et al.\cite{WangCycle2022}; Huang et al.\cite{HuangTreadmill2024};
PUTH equation\cite{PUTHCycle2025}) provide valuable local data but are
typically single-equation models not accounting for age--protocol interactions.
The FRIEND series\cite{FRIEND2016cycle,FRIEND2022} established a quantile
reference framework but lacks conditional quantile modelling for elderly
hypertensive subgroups with EIH.

\textbf{Gap~3: Absence of explicit uncertainty handling.}
Summary-level CPET data have structural information ceilings: inadequate effort,
missing core fields, and inconsistent external--internal reference agreement all
reduce stratification reliability. Existing frameworks force definitive
classifications without distinguishing ``high-confidence Green from 6 complete
core fields'' from ``Green by default due to 3 missing fields.''
Prediction model reporting standards (TRIPOD+AI\cite{TRIPODAI2024};
PROBAST+AI\cite{PROBASTAI2025}) explicitly require uncertainty declaration.

This study addresses all three gaps by constructing a Stage~1B laboratory
safety phenotype prototype from 3,206 elderly hypertensive patients' CPET
summary data, replacing single-equation reference normalization with conditional
quantile modelling, mixed composite scoring with dual-domain phenotype burden
assessment, and forced classification with confidence-driven uncertainty output.

\subsection{Variable Role Assignment}

All CPET variables were assigned to one of four roles to prevent construct
contamination and data leakage:

\textbf{(1) Phenotype variables} (for burden scoring): Reserve domain: VO$_2$peak,
VT1-VO$_2$, O$_2$ pulse peak, peak METs; Ventilatory domain:
$V_E/V_{\text{CO}_2}$ slope, OUES.

\textbf{(2) Instability variables} (for override rules; excluded from phenotype score):
EIH status, bp\_peak\_sys, bp\_peak\_dia, O$_2$ pulse trajectory proxy.

\textbf{(3) Validation variables} (for external anchoring only; do not define zones):
test\_result, group\_code.

\textbf{(4) Excluded variables} (legacy comparison only):
zone\_v2, p1\_zone, S\_lab.

\subsection{Reference-Normal Subset}

The strict reference subset is defined as CTRL cohort with VO$_2$peak\%pred
$\geq$70\%, $V_E/V_{\text{CO}_2}$ slope $\leq$30, no EIH, peak HR
$\geq$85\% maximum predicted HR. The wide reference subset (CTRL only) is used
for sensitivity analyses.

\subsection{Conditional Quantile Reference Models}

Quantile regression models (sklearn QuantileRegressor) were fitted on the strict
reference subset at $\tau \in \{0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 0.90\}$:

\begin{equation}
Q_\tau(Y \mid \mathbf{z}) =
\text{QuantileRegressor}\bigl(
  \text{SplineTransformer}(\text{age}) \oplus
  \text{OneHot}(\text{sex}, \text{protocol})
\bigr)
\end{equation}

SplineTransformer captures non-linear age effects (knots=5);
OneHotEncoder handles sex (M/F) and protocol (cycle/treadmill).
Post-processing via \texttt{np.sort} enforces monotonicity.

\subsection{Phenotype Burden Scoring}

\subsubsection{Variable-Level Burden Conversion}

Each phenotype variable is converted to a 0/0.5/1.0 burden based on its
conditional quantile reference:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Higher-is-better (VO$_2$peak, VT1-VO$_2$, O$_2$ pulse, METs):
    burden=0 ($\geq$q25); burden=0.5 (q10--q25); burden=1.0 ($<$q10).
  \item Higher-is-worse ($V_E/V_{\text{CO}_2}$ slope):
    burden=0 ($\leq$q75); burden=0.5 (q75--q90); burden=1.0 ($>$q90).
\end{itemize}

Domain aggregation requires Reserve $\geq$2 available fields,
Ventilatory $\geq$1 field.

\subsubsection{Domain Aggregation and Phenotype Zone Assignment}

\begin{equation}
P_{\text{lab}} = 0.5 \times \text{Reserve\_burden} + 0.5 \times \text{Ventilatory\_burden}
\end{equation}

Cutpoints from the P75 and P90 of $P_{\text{lab}}$ on the strict reference subset:
\begin{equation}
\text{phenotype\_zone} =
\begin{cases}
\text{green}  & P_{\text{lab}} \leq P_{75}^{\text{ref}} \\
\text{yellow} & P_{75}^{\text{ref}} < P_{\text{lab}} \leq P_{90}^{\text{ref}} \\
\text{red}    & P_{\text{lab}} > P_{90}^{\text{ref}}
\end{cases}
\end{equation}

Equal-domain weighting eliminates dependence on parameter estimation and
outcome-driven weight optimisation.

\subsection{Instability Override Rules}

Instability signals are acute safety events and are processed independently of
chronic phenotype burden.

\textbf{Severe rules} (any $\rightarrow$ force Red; cannot be overridden by
confidence engine): EIH=True; bp\_peak\_sys $>$220~mmHg;
bp\_peak\_dia $>$110~mmHg; abnormal O$_2$ pulse trajectory.

\textbf{Mild rules} (Green $\rightarrow$ Yellow only; no downgrade):
200 $<$ bp\_peak\_sys $\leq$ 220~mmHg; effort uncertain (HR\%pred $<$ 85\%).

\subsection{Confidence Engine}

\begin{equation}
C = 0.40 \times C_{\text{completeness}} + 0.15 \times C_{\text{effort}}
    + 0.20 \times C_{\text{anchor}} + 0.25 \times C_{\text{validation}}
\end{equation}

Labels: $C \geq 0.75$ = high; $0.60 \leq C < 0.75$ = medium; $C < 0.60$ = low.

Final zone:
\begin{equation}
\text{final\_zone} =
\begin{cases}
\text{red}          & \text{severe instability (confidence-invariant)} \\
\text{yellow\_gray} & C < 0.60 \wedge \neg\text{severe} \quad
                      \text{(indeterminate)} \\
\text{pre-confidence zone} & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Outcome-Anchor Validation Model}

An ElasticNet logistic regression (solver=SAGA, L1-ratio=0.5; nested 5-fold CV)
and LightGBM (sensitivity) predict test\_result as a binary outcome.
Evaluation: CV AUC (mean$\pm$SD), test AUC, AUPRC, Brier score, calibration.
\textbf{The outcome-anchor model does not define zones}; it provides
outcome\_risk\_tertile for the confidence engine and validates the directional
relationship between final\_zone and clinical outcome.

\subsection{Anomaly Phenotype Audit}

Robust Mahalanobis distance\cite{Mahalanobis1936} (MinCovDet; $\chi^2_{k,0.975}$
threshold) on the reference subset identifies outlier inputs.
anomaly\_flag is for QC use only (\textbf{not used in zone definition}).

\subsection{Statistical Analysis}

Baseline comparisons: Kruskal-Wallis test and $\chi^2$ test with Dunn's post-hoc
(Bonferroni, $\alpha$=0.0083). HTN$\times$EIH interaction: Type~II ANOVA
(statsmodels), effect size $\eta^2$\cite{Cohen1988}.
Construct validity: monotone gradient of final\_zone against test\_result
positive rates. Robustness: sex-, age-, protocol-, and BP-availability-stratified
analyses. Reported per TRIPOD+AI\cite{TRIPODAI2024}.

\section{Discussion}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Finding 1: A Single Supervised Label Is Insufficient for the
Safety Construct}

The central methodological contribution of this study is precisely delineating
the construct boundaries of summary-level CPET data. Chronic reserve impairment
and acute hemodynamic instability are fundamentally distinct risk mechanisms
that should not be mixed into a single weighted composite, nor should
test\_result be used to reverse-derive zone boundaries\cite{Ross2024}.
When zone definitions are optimised against a supervised label, the model
effectively learns direct predictors of test\_result (including EIH and peak
blood pressure), which should instead be handled as independent override signals.
Phase~G outcome-anchored model AUC (0.548) approximating the 2-variable clinical
baseline (HTN+BMI, AUC=0.611) empirically confirms the information ceiling of
summary-level data under the supervised prediction path.
\textit{This study does not misrepresent summary-level CPET as a high-precision
predictor, but explicitly positions it as a laboratory prototype to define
identifiable endpoints and indeterminate zones.}

\subsection{Finding 2: Dual-Anchor Conditional Quantile Reference Outperforms
Single-Equation OLS}

External equation failure ($R^2<0$, maximum bias $-$11.85~mL/kg/min) not only
demonstrates the necessity of local reference normalization but exposes the
inability of single-point OLS equations to capture distributional tail
differences. Conditional quantile modelling (q10/q25/q50/q75/q90) captures
non-linear age--protocol interactions, enabling meaningful separation between
``severely below reference'' ($<$q10) and ``borderline'' (q10--q25).
This approach aligns with and extends Chinese reference initiatives
(X-ET\cite{XET2021}; Wang et al.\cite{WangCycle2022}) and the FRIEND
quantile framework\cite{FRIEND2022} to protocol-specific conditional modelling.
\textit{test\_result was used for external anchoring and construct validation,
not for reverse-deriving the primary zone labels.}

\subsection{Finding 3: Instability Signals Must Be Processed Independently
of Chronic Phenotype}

ANOVA confirmed that HTN primarily affects the Reserve domain
($\eta^2$=0.084, VO$_2$peak) while EIH primarily affects acute hemodynamics
($\eta^2$=0.371, peak SBP), with no significant interaction on VO$_2$peak
($p$=0.894). This statistical structure provides physiological justification for
the dual-layer architecture: instability signals (EIH, hypertensive peak response)
cannot be ``cancelled'' by a strong Reserve component---a risk present in
outcome-driven composites such as the CPX score\cite{Gorodeski2008cpx}
that is eliminated by structural design in Stage~1B.
\textit{We separated acute instability signals from chronic phenotype burden
in modelling to prevent construct contamination.}

\subsection{Finding 4: Uncertainty Output Is the Most Important Stage~1B
Contribution}

In summary-level CPET data, inadequate effort, missing core fields, and
inconsistent external--internal reference agreement are ubiquitous data quality
issues. Forcing deterministic classification conceals the fundamental difference
between ``high-confidence Green from 6 complete core fields'' and ``Green by
default from 3 missing fields.'' Stage~1B's yellow\_gray indeterminate zone
is itself a core output, not a methodological limitation. This design is
consistent with TRIPOD+AI\cite{TRIPODAI2024} and PROBAST+AI\cite{PROBASTAI2025}
requirements for explicit uncertainty declaration in clinical prediction models.
Notably, the high indeterminate-zone prevalence observed (1.9\% vs expected 10--25\%)
reflects the high data completeness of this cohort (core variable non-null rate $>$97\%),
not overly liberal thresholds. In real-world cohorts with more frequent field
missingness, the indeterminate proportion would be expected to increase substantially.
\textit{Preserving the indeterminate zone is an intentional design feature of this
study, not an analytic failure.}

\subsection{Limitations}

(1) Single-centre retrospective design; no independent external validation cohort.
(2) Medication information completely absent ($\beta$-blocker effects on HR proxy).
(3) EIH defined by group coding rather than continuous SpO$_2$ monitoring.
(4) Confidence weights (0.40/0.15/0.20/0.25) derived from methodological priors,
not prospective calibration.
(5) No breath-by-breath waveforms; VT1/RCP cannot be precisely detected.
(6) test\_result is a summary-level proxy variable (technician composite judgement),
not a prospectively collected safety event.
(7) No follow-up outcome data; the long-term prognostic value of final\_zone
requires validation in a prospective cohort with true outcomes.
(8) The structural information ceiling of summary-level data limits the statistical
power of construct validity gradient testing; non-monotone results are an expected
consequence of this constraint, not a framework design defect.
Per PROBAST+AI\cite{PROBASTAI2025}, Stage~1B is positioned as a Stage~I laboratory
methodological tool, not a deployable clinical prediction instrument.

\subsection{Future Directions}

Stage~II research should incorporate breath-by-breath gas exchange and 12-lead ECG
waveform data\cite{Hearn2018,Zignoli2022ejss}. Multi-centre prospective validation
should include complete medication information. Stage~1B's yellow\_gray indeterminate
zone will serve as the primary recruitment gate for Stage~II waveform analysis,
filling the data gap where information is most needed.
\textbf{Most critically}, a prospective follow-up study should use real clinical
outcomes (adverse cardiovascular events, emergency stops, hospitalisations) as the
external criterion to independently validate Stage~1B's predictive construct
validity\cite{MECKI2023}. This step is a prerequisite for transitioning from
a methodological prototype to a clinical decision support tool.

% ============================================================
\section{Conclusion}
% ============================================================

This study constructs the first laboratory safety phenotype prototype from
summary-level CPET data that: (1) replaces single-point OLS reference equations
with conditional quantile modelling for distribution-aware variable-level burden
scoring; (2) strictly separates chronic reserve impairment (dual-domain phenotype
burden) from acute hemodynamic instability (independent override rules);
(3) explicitly outputs an indeterminate zone (yellow\_gray) for low-confidence
samples via a confidence engine, rather than forcing classification;
and (4) validates directional construct validity through an external outcome-anchor
model without using test\_result to define zone boundaries.

Stage~1B provides a methodologically reproducible safety stratification prototype
for Chinese elderly hypertensive patients' CPET evaluation, positioned as a
Stage~II waveform-level research entry-point definition tool.
