\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Background and Objective:}
Summary-level cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET) data encode three
heterogeneous risk constructs: chronic reserve impairment, ventilatory
inefficiency, and acute haemodynamic instability. Conflating these constructs
into a single supervised label introduces construct conflation and circularity.
Existing reference equations derived from Western populations have systematic
applicability limitations in Chinese elderly hypertensive patients, while existing
frameworks force definitive classifications without explicit uncertainty handling.
This study aimed to construct a safety stratification framework from summary-level
CPET data in 3,232 elderly hypertensive patients (2015--2023), using phenotype
burden scoring as the core layer, instability override rules as the safety layer,
and explicit uncertainty quantification via a confidence engine.

\textbf{Methods:}
Single-centre retrospective cohort study (N=3,232) with a hypertension history (HTN)
$\times$ exercise-induced hypertension (EHT) 2$\times$2 design. The analytical
framework comprises four methodological layers:
(1) Conditional quantile reference modelling (natural cubic spline for age
+ sex + protocol; $\tau \in \{0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 0.90\}$);
(2) Dual-domain phenotype burden scoring (Reserve domain: VO$_2$peak, VT1-VO$_2$,
O$_2$ pulse, peak METs; Ventilatory domain: $V_E/V_{\text{CO}_2}$ slope, OUES;
three-level burden per variable; equal-weight domain aggregation);
(3) Instability override rules (severe instability forces high-risk;
mild instability upgrades low-risk to intermediate-risk);
(4) Four-component confidence engine (field completeness, effort proxy,
reference agreement, outcome agreement; low-confidence samples output as
indeterminate zone). An ElasticNet logistic regression (nested cross-validation)
was used for external construct validity anchoring.

\textbf{Results:}
Local conditional quantile reference modelling improved VO$_2$peak $R^2$
from 0.298 to 0.392 and O$_2$ pulse $R^2$ from 0.200 to 0.511;
Western reference equations showed $R^2 < 0$ (maximum bias $-$11.85~mL/kg/min).
Reference-normal subset (N=903): low-risk 69.2\%, high-risk 10.2\%
(pre-specified quality criteria satisfied).
Full sample (N=3,232) final safety stratification: low-risk 19.7\%,
intermediate-risk 25.6\%, high-risk 29.7\%, indeterminate zone 24.9\%;
high-confidence proportion 33.0\%.
Construct validity: a confirmed monotone gradient of safety strata against
clinical outcome proxy positive rates (low-risk 14.0\% $<$ intermediate-risk 15.1\%
$<$ high-risk 18.0\%; Cochran-Armitage trend $\chi^2 = 5.04$, $p = 0.025$),
meeting pre-specified quality criteria.
Outcome-anchor validator cross-validated AUC 0.564 ($\pm$0.016), test AUC 0.608,
consistent with the expected summary-level CPET information ceiling (0.55--0.65).

\textbf{Conclusions:}
This study establishes a summary-level CPET safety stratification framework
that strictly separates chronic reserve impairment from acute instability signals
and replaces forced classification with explicit uncertainty quantification,
providing a reproducible CPET safety assessment methodology for Chinese
elderly hypertensive patients and a target population screening entry-point
for subsequent breath-by-breath waveform-level research.

\textbf{Keywords:} cardiopulmonary exercise testing; phenotype burden score;
exercise safety stratification; instability override; confidence quantification;
uncertainty output; conditional quantile reference; Chinese hypertension
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% ============================================================

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) is the leading cause of disability and death in
elderly populations worldwide\cite{WHO2023}. Exercise-based cardiac rehabilitation
demonstrates clear benefit in CVD outcomes\cite{Piepoli2016,Thomas2019}, yet
exercise-induced adverse events---particularly haemodynamic instability
(exercise-induced hypertension, hypertensive crises)---limit the precise
prescribing of exercise for elderly hypertensive patients\cite{Myers2013}.
Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET) is the gold standard for exercise safety
assessment\cite{Balady2010,Guazzi2012}, with its safety in Chinese clinical
populations confirmed by large-scale studies\cite{Xiangya2021,SunXG2022consensus}.
Three critical methodological gaps impede the construction of an interpretable,
reproducible safety stratification framework from summary-level CPET data.

Construct contamination is the primary methodological challenge.
Exercise safety encompasses two fundamentally distinct risk mechanisms:
chronic reserve impairment (phenotype burden) and acute haemodynamic instability
(override signals). Combining these into a single composite score, or using the
clinical outcome proxy as a primary label to derive zone boundaries, creates
construct conflation and circularity\cite{Ross2024}.
Existing CPET composite risk scores (CPX score\cite{Gorodeski2008cpx,CPXvalidation2013};
MECKI\cite{MECKI2023}) demonstrate prognostic value but are derived from
heart failure populations and outcome-driven regression, limiting direct
transferability to the exercise safety stratification context in Chinese
elderly hypertensive patients.

Population-specific reference limitation is the second challenge.
Western reference equations (Wasserman \& Hansen 1999\cite{Wasserman1999};
Koch et al. 2009\cite{Koch2009}) fail systematically in Chinese elderly
hypertensive patients ($R^2 < 0$, maximum bias $-$11.85~mL/kg/min).
Chinese reference initiatives (X-ET Project\cite{XET2021};
Wang et al.\cite{WangCycle2022}; Huang et al.\cite{HuangTreadmill2024};
PUTH equation\cite{PUTHCycle2025}) provide valuable local data but are
typically single-equation models not accounting for age--protocol interactions.
The FRIEND series\cite{FRIEND2016cycle,FRIEND2022} established a quantile
reference framework but lacks conditional quantile modelling for elderly
hypertensive subgroups with EHT.

Absence of explicit uncertainty handling is the third gap.
Summary-level CPET data have structural information ceilings: inadequate effort,
missing core fields, and inconsistent external--internal reference agreement all
reduce stratification reliability. Existing frameworks force definitive
classifications without distinguishing ``high-confidence low-risk from 6
complete core fields'' from ``low-risk by default due to 3 missing fields.''
Prediction model reporting standards (TRIPOD+AI\cite{TRIPODAI2024};
PROBAST+AI\cite{PROBASTAI2025}) explicitly require uncertainty declaration.

This study addresses all three gaps using 3,232 elderly hypertensive patients'
CPET summary data, replacing single-equation reference normalization with
conditional quantile modelling, mixed composite scoring with three-level burden
conversion, and forced classification with confidence-driven explicit uncertainty
output. The framework is positioned as a methodological tool,
providing target population screening entry-points for subsequent breath-by-breath
waveform-level research.

\subsection{Variable Role Assignment}

\textbf{Clinical outcome proxy} (operational definition): the overall exercise
test result (positive/equivocal/negative) assigned by the supervising physician
based on a composite assessment of ECG changes (ST depression, ventricular
arrhythmia), haemodynamic response (abnormal peak blood pressure rise or fall),
symptoms (chest pain, dyspnoea), and exercise tolerance. Positive and equivocal
results were merged as ``positive'' to form a binary outcome. The clinical outcome
proxy is used only as an external validation variable \emph{after} final safety
strata are defined; it must not be used to derive stratum boundaries.

All CPET variables were assigned to one of four roles to prevent construct
contamination (Table~\ref{tab:variable_roles_en}).

\subsection{Reference-Normal Subset}

The strict reference subset is defined as CTRL cohort with VO$_2$peak\%pred
$\geq$70\%, $V_E/V_{\text{CO}_2}$ slope $\leq$30, no EHT, and peak HR
$\geq$85\% of maximum predicted HR (N $\approx$ 900). The wide reference subset
(CTRL only, N $\approx$ 969) serves for sensitivity analyses. The strict subset
is used for conditional quantile model fitting and phenotype burden cutpoint
estimation.

\subsection{Conditional Quantile Reference Models}

Conditional quantile regression models were fitted on the strict reference
subset at $\tau \in \{0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 0.90\}$.
The covariate vector concatenates a natural cubic spline basis for age with
sex and protocol indicator variables:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:quantile_en}
Q_\tau(Y \mid \mathbf{z}) = \boldsymbol{\beta}_\tau^\top
\bigl[\mathbf{s}(\text{age};\, K{=}5) \oplus \mathbf{d}_{\text{sex}}
\oplus \mathbf{d}_{\text{protocol}}\bigr]
\end{equation}

where $\mathbf{s}(\cdot;\, K)$ denotes the natural cubic spline basis
($K$ interior knots capturing non-linear age effects), $\mathbf{d}$ are
indicator variables, and $\oplus$ denotes vector concatenation.
Parameters $\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}_\tau$ are estimated by minimising the
pinball loss:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pinball_en}
\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}_\tau = \arg\min_{\boldsymbol{\beta}}
\sum_{i=1}^{n} \rho_\tau\bigl(Y_i - \boldsymbol{\beta}^\top \mathbf{z}_i\bigr),
\quad \rho_\tau(u) = u\bigl(\tau - \mathbf{1}_{u < 0}\bigr)
\end{equation}

Monotonicity is enforced by sorting the five quantile predictions for each
sample, ensuring $\hat{Q}_{0.10} \leq \hat{Q}_{0.25} \leq \hat{Q}_{0.50}
\leq \hat{Q}_{0.75} \leq \hat{Q}_{0.90}$. Cycle and treadmill protocols
each have independent conditional reference curves.

\subsubsection{Domain Aggregation and Phenotype Zone Assignment}

Reserve domain burden $B_R$ is the within-domain mean of four Reserve variable
burdens; Ventilatory domain burden $B_V$ is the within-domain mean of two
Ventilatory variable burdens. The composite phenotype burden score:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:plab_en}
P_{\text{lab}} = 0.5 \times B_R + 0.5 \times B_V
\end{equation}

$P_{\text{lab}} \in [0, 1]$ (0 = no burden; 1 = maximum burden). Equal-weight
domain design eliminates dependence on outcome-driven parameter optimisation.
Cutpoints from the P75 and P90 of $P_{\text{lab}}$ on the strict reference
subset define the phenotype stratum $Z_{\text{ph}}$:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:phenotype_zone_en}
Z_{\text{ph}} =
\begin{cases}
\text{low-risk}          & P_{\text{lab}} \leq P_{75}^{\text{ref}} \\
\text{intermediate-risk} & P_{75}^{\text{ref}} < P_{\text{lab}} \leq P_{90}^{\text{ref}} \\
\text{high-risk}         & P_{\text{lab}} > P_{90}^{\text{ref}}
\end{cases}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Instability Override Rules}

Instability signals are acute safety events and are processed independently of
chronic phenotype burden (Table~\ref{tab:instability_rules_en}).
Thresholds are derived from AHA exercise testing guidelines\cite{Fletcher2013}
and ESC sports cardiology guidelines\cite{Pelliccia2022}.

\begin{tabular}{llll}

\midrule

 & Peak DBP & $>$110 mmHg & Same \\
 & O$_2$ pulse trajectory & Abnormal (proxy $<$0) & Same \\
\midrule
Mild instability & Peak SBP & 200--220 mmHg & Upgrade low-risk $\to$ intermediate (no downgrade) \\

The pre-confidence stratum $Z_{\text{pre}}$ is:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pre_confidence_zone_en}
Z_{\text{pre}} =
\begin{cases}
\text{high-risk}         & \text{severe instability} = \text{True} \\
\text{intermediate-risk} & \text{mild instability} = \text{True}
                           \wedge Z_{\text{ph}} = \text{low-risk} \\
Z_{\text{ph}}            & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Confidence Engine}

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:confidence_en}
C = w_1 C_{\text{completeness}} + w_2 C_{\text{effort}}
    + w_3 C_{\text{anchor}} + w_4 C_{\text{validation}}
\end{equation}

Weights $(w_1, w_2, w_3, w_4) = (0.25, 0.20, 0.25, 0.30)$. Component
definitions are given in Table~\ref{tab:confidence_components_en}. Labels:
$C \geq 0.80$ = high; $0.65 \leq C < 0.80$ = medium; $C < 0.65$ = low.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\small
\caption{Confidence Component Definitions}
\label{tab:confidence_components_en}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Component & Meaning & Computation \\
\midrule
$C_{\text{completeness}}$ & Core field completeness rate & Proportion of phenotype + instability + diagnostic fields present \\
$C_{\text{effort}}$ & Effort proxy & HR\%pred $\geq$85\%$\to$1.0; 70--85\%$\to$0.5; $<$70\%$\to$0.0 \\
$C_{\text{anchor}}$ & External--internal reference agreement & Agreement of external equation vs internal quantile on VO$_2$peak grade \\
$C_{\text{validation}}$ & Outcome directional agreement & Agreement of $Z_{\text{pre}}$ with outcome risk tertile direction \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\\[1ex]
{\footnotesize Weights are based on methodological priors and require prospective calibration.}
\end{table}

Final safety stratum $Z_{\text{final}}$:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:final_zone_en}
Z_{\text{final}} =
\begin{cases}
\text{high-risk}      & \text{severe instability} = \text{True}
                        \quad \text{(confidence-invariant)} \\
\text{indeterminate}  & C < 0.65 \wedge \neg\,\text{severe instability} \\
Z_{\text{pre}}        & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Outcome-Anchor Validation Model}

An ElasticNet logistic regression (solver=SAGA, L1-ratio=0.5; nested 5-fold CV)
predicts the clinical outcome proxy as a binary outcome. Evaluation: CV AUC
(mean$\pm$SD), test AUC, AUPRC, Brier score. \textbf{The outcome-anchor model
does not define strata}; it provides outcome risk tertiles for the confidence engine
and validates the directional relationship between safety strata and clinical outcome proxy.

\subsection{Anomaly Phenotype Audit}

Robust Mahalanobis distance\cite{Mahalanobis1936} (Minimum Covariance Determinant;
$\chi^2_{k,0.975}$ threshold) applied to the reference subset identifies outlier
inputs. The anomaly flag is used for data quality control only and \textbf{does
not contribute to safety stratum assignment}.

\subsection{Statistical Analysis}

Baseline comparisons: Kruskal-Wallis test and $\chi^2$ test with Dunn's post-hoc
(Bonferroni, $\alpha$=0.0083). HTN$\times$EHT interaction: Type~II ANOVA
(statsmodels), partial effect size $\eta^2_p$\cite{Cohen1988}.
Construct validity: monotone gradient of safety strata against clinical outcome
proxy positive rates (low-risk $<$ intermediate-risk $<$ high-risk), formally tested
by Cochran-Armitage trend test (ordered categories, linear trend).
Robustness: sex-, age-, protocol-, and BP-availability-stratified analyses.
Reported per TRIPOD+AI\cite{TRIPODAI2024}.

\subsection{Physiological Basis for Dual-Domain Separation}

Type~II ANOVA (Table~\ref{tab:anova_en}) confirmed that HTN and EHT act on
distinct cardiopulmonary dimensions. HTN was the main driver of VO$_2$peak
reduction ($\eta^2_p = 0.084$, medium effect) and peak HR decline
($\eta^2_p = 0.133$). EHT primarily drove peak SBP elevation
($\eta^2_p = 0.371$, large effect), with minimal independent effect on VO$_2$peak
($\eta^2_p = 0.001$). The HTN$\times$EHT interaction on absolute VO$_2$peak was
non-significant ($p = 0.894$), consistent with the interpretation that both factors
exert independent additive effects on aerobic reserve---a statistical structure that
directly supports processing the Reserve domain and the Instability override layer
separately. The significant interaction on VO$_2$peak\%pred ($p = 0.025$) suggests
a modest synergistic effect between HTN and EHT on percentage-predicted values.

\subsection{Conditional Quantile Reference Modelling and External Reference Comparison}

The conditional quantile model fitted on the strict reference subset satisfied
monotonicity constraints for all six phenotype variables, with independent
reference curves established for cycle and treadmill protocols.

External reference equations\cite{Wasserman1999,Koch2009} showed $R^2 < 0$
in this population (maximum bias $-$11.85~mL/kg/min), confirming their
inapplicability. The local conditional quantile reference model increased
VO$_2$peak $R^2$ from 0.298 to 0.392 ($\Delta R^2$=+0.094) and O$_2$
pulse $R^2$ from 0.200 to 0.511 (Table~\ref{tab:refequations_en});
the magnitude of improvement is consistent with reports from Chinese reference
initiatives (X-ET\cite{XET2021}; Wang et al.\cite{WangCycle2022};
FRIEND series\cite{FRIEND2022}).

\subsection{Safety Stratification Results and Construct Validity}

\subsubsection{Phenotype Burden Stratum Distribution}

Full sample (N=3,232) phenotype burden strata: low-risk 30.7\% (992),
intermediate-risk 27.1\% (877), high-risk 41.0\% (1,326), missing 1.1\% (37).
$P_{\text{lab}}$ mean 0.420 (SD 0.313), median 0.375 (P25/P75: 0.125/0.700).
Reference-normal subset (N=903): low-risk 69.2\%, high-risk 10.2\%,
satisfying pre-specified quality criteria (low-risk $>$50\%, high-risk $<$15\%).
$P_{\text{lab}}$ was significantly lower in CTRL versus HTN-EHT/EHT-Only cohorts
(group VO$_2$peak differences shown in Figure~\ref{fig:interaction_en}),
consistent with VO$_2$peak and $V_E/V_{\text{CO}_2}$ slope group differences,
indicating that the phenotype burden score effectively discriminates across
cardiopulmonary functional states.

\subsubsection{Instability Override Effects}

Severe instability triggered in 220 samples (6.8\%), forced to high-risk;
mild instability (non-severe) in 315 (9.7\%), of which 243 (7.5\%) were
upgraded from low-risk to intermediate-risk. After override application,
the pre-confidence stratum distribution was: low-risk 25.0\%, intermediate-risk 29.0\%,
high-risk 44.9\% (+3.9 percentage points relative to phenotype-only high-risk).
High-risk source decomposition: instability-override high-risk 220 (6.8\%)
and phenotype-burden high-risk 741 (22.9\%)---these two sources are
mechanistically distinct, with the former driven primarily by EHT and peak
hypertensive response and the latter by low aerobic reserve capacity.
HTN-EHT and EHT-Only cohorts had the highest severe-trigger rates, consistent
with their baseline haemodynamic profile (safety stratum distributions shown in
Figure~\ref{fig:zone_dist_en}).

\subsubsection{Confidence Calibration and Indeterminate Zone Output}

High confidence ($C \geq 0.80$): 1,065 (33.0\%); medium confidence
($0.65 \leq C < 0.80$): 1,331 (41.2\%); low confidence ($C < 0.65$):
836 (25.9\%); low-confidence non-severe samples were assigned to the
indeterminate zone (N=805, 24.9\%).
Final safety strata (post-confidence): low-risk 19.7\%, intermediate-risk 25.6\%,
high-risk 29.7\%, indeterminate 24.9\%. Inadequate effort (HR\%pred $<$85\%)
and outcome directional agreement were the main sources of confidence loss.
The indeterminate zone (24.9\%) precisely quantifies the methodological boundary
of the current analytical framework under summary-level data conditions,
representing a principled acknowledgement of non-classifiable samples
(Table~\ref{tab:confidence_layers_en}; confidence and construct validity shown
in Figure~\ref{fig:confidence_en}).

\subsubsection{Construct Validity Gradient}

The final safety strata exhibited a confirmed monotone gradient against

test AUC 0.608, AUPRC 0.203, Brier Score 0.125---all within the expected
information ceiling for summary-level CPET (0.55--0.65).

\subsection{Robustness Analysis}

Stratified analyses by sex, age ($<$67/$\geq$67 years), protocol
(cycle/treadmill), and BP field availability confirmed that primary quality
criteria (reference low-risk $>$50\%, high-risk $<$15\%, monotone gradient)
held consistently. Alternative confidence weight schemes (equal weights,
completeness-prioritised, and no-outcome-component) yielded the same gradient
direction. Compared with prior scoring approaches based on outcome-driven
continuous scores, the present framework offers three methodological improvements:
elimination of outcome proxy influence on stratum boundary definition;
separation of acute instability signals from chronic phenotype burden scoring;
and replacement of forced classification for low-confidence samples with an
explicit indeterminate zone. Detailed stratified validation results are presented
in the Supplementary Material.

% ============================================================
\section{Discussion}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Construct Separation as a Methodological Foundation}

The central methodological contribution of this study is precisely delineating
the construct boundaries of summary-level CPET data. Chronic reserve impairment
($P_{\text{lab}}$) and acute haemodynamic instability are fundamentally distinct
risk mechanisms that must not be mixed into a single weighted composite, nor
should the clinical outcome proxy be used to reverse-derive stratum
boundaries\cite{Ross2024}. When zone definitions are optimised against a
supervised label, the model effectively learns direct predictors of the outcome
proxy (including EHT and peak blood pressure), which should instead be processed
as independent override signals---absorbing them into a composite score
``averages away'' high-risk signals.
The outcome-anchor validator CV AUC (0.564) was lower than the two-variable
clinical baseline (HTN+BMI, AUC=0.611), empirically confirming the information
ceiling of summary-level data under the supervised prediction path.

It should be noted that the performance gap between this framework and CPX
score\cite{Gorodeski2008cpx} (heart failure cohorts, AUC=0.77--0.82) or MECKI
score\cite{MECKI2023} (C-statistic 0.73) reflects differences in target (safety
stratification vs.\ all-cause mortality prediction) and data level (summary-level
vs.\ complete breath-by-breath CPET) rather than methodological deficiency.
Under the same data conditions and the same target, the summary-level information
ceiling observed here (0.55--0.65) is precisely consistent with the design,
demonstrating that the observed performance faithfully reflects the information
boundary of summary-level CPET data, rather than any methodological deficiency.

\subsection{Population-Specific Conditional Quantile Reference Modelling}

External equation failure ($R^2<0$, maximum bias $-$11.85~mL/kg/min) not only
demonstrates the necessity of local reference normalisation but exposes the
inability of single-point OLS equations to capture distributional tail
differences. Conditional quantile modelling
($\tau \in \{0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 0.90\}$) captures non-linear
age--protocol interactions, enabling meaningful separation between ``severely
below reference'' ($<$q10) and ``borderline'' (q10--q25)---a distinction
unavailable with single-cutpoint methods.
This approach aligns with and extends Chinese reference initiatives
(X-ET\cite{XET2021}; Wang et al.\cite{WangCycle2022}) and the FRIEND quantile
framework\cite{FRIEND2022} to protocol-specific conditional modelling.
The magnitude of VO$_2$peak $R^2$ improvement ($\Delta R^2$=+0.094) is
consistent with the range reported by Chinese reference studies, further
substantiating the methodological value of conditional quantile reference
construction in elderly hypertensive populations.

\subsection{Independent Processing of Instability Signals}

ANOVA confirmed that HTN primarily affects the Reserve domain
($\eta^2_p = 0.084$, VO$_2$peak) while EHT primarily affects acute haemodynamics
($\eta^2_p = 0.371$, peak SBP), with no significant interaction on absolute VO$_2$peak
($p = 0.894$), consistent with independent additive effects on aerobic reserve. This
statistical structure provides physiological justification for
the dual-layer architecture: instability signals (EHT, hypertensive peak response)
are processed by override rules and cannot be ``cancelled'' by strong reserve
capacity---a risk inherent in outcome-driven composite scores that is eliminated
by structural design in the present framework.
The clear decomposition of high-risk sources---instability-override high-risk
(220 cases, 6.8\%) versus phenotype-burden high-risk (741 cases, 22.9\%)---provides
mechanistically differentiated targets for clinical intervention: the former
requires priority management of acute safety risk (e.g., exercise intensity or
protocol adjustment), while the latter necessitates systematic improvement of
aerobic reserve capacity.

\subsection{Explicit Quantification of Uncertainty}

In summary-level CPET data, inadequate effort, missing core fields, and
inconsistent external--internal reference agreement are pervasive data quality
issues. Forcing deterministic classification conceals the fundamental difference
between ``high-confidence low-risk from 6 complete core fields'' and ``low-risk
by default from 3 missing fields.'' The indeterminate zone (24.9\%) explicitly
marks low-confidence non-severe samples---this is not an analytic failure but a
principled acknowledgement of the limits of available information, fully
consistent with TRIPOD+AI\cite{TRIPODAI2024} and PROBAST+AI\cite{PROBASTAI2025}
requirements for transparency in clinical prediction models.
The confidence stratification itself carries clinical significance: the
high-confidence subset (33.0\%) shows the highest outcome-proxy positive rate
(16.4\%), while medium-confidence follows (15.4\%), identifying high-confidence
high-risk patients as the priority target for exercise safety intervention and
indeterminate-zone patients as the group most needing supplementary data
collection (e.g., breath-by-breath waveform monitoring) and the priority
recruitment source for subsequent waveform-level research.

\subsection{Limitations}

(1) Single-centre retrospective design with no independent external validation
cohort; generalisability requires multi-centre prospective confirmation.
(2) Medication information is entirely absent; $\beta$-blocker effects may
systematically lower the effort proxy score, biasing confidence allocation.
(3) EHT is defined by cohort coding rather than continuous intra-exercise blood
pressure monitoring, limiting ascertainment precision.
(4) Confidence weights are based on methodological priors and have not been
calibrated on prospectively labelled data; optimal weights require future
data-driven determination.
(5) No breath-by-breath waveforms are available; ventilatory threshold and
respiratory compensation point cannot be precisely detected.
(6) The clinical outcome proxy is a summary-level technician composite
judgement, not a prospectively collected safety event, and cannot substitute
for true outcome validation.
(7) No follow-up outcome data are available; the long-term prognostic value
of safety strata requires validation in a prospective cohort with real outcomes.
Per PROBAST+AI\cite{PROBASTAI2025}, this framework is positioned as a
laboratory methodological tool, not a deployable clinical prediction instrument.

\subsection{Future Directions}

Subsequent research should incorporate breath-by-breath gas exchange and 12-lead
ECG waveform data\cite{Hearn2018,Zignoli2022ejss} to overcome the information
ceiling of summary-level data. Multi-centre prospective validation should include
complete medication information to calibrate systematic effort-proxy bias.
The indeterminate zone defined by the present framework will serve as the primary
recruitment gate for waveform-level analysis, precisely targeting the samples
where additional information is most needed.
Most critically, a prospective follow-up study using real clinical outcomes
(adverse cardiovascular events, emergency exercise stops, hospitalisations) as
the external criterion should independently validate the predictive construct
validity of the safety strata\cite{MECKI2023}---a prerequisite for transitioning
from a methodological framework to a clinical decision support tool.

% ============================================================
\section{Conclusion}
% ============================================================

This study establishes a safety stratification framework from summary-level CPET
data in 3,232 elderly hypertensive patients that strictly separates chronic reserve
phenotype from acute instability signals and replaces forced classification with
explicit uncertainty quantification, with construct validity confirmed through a
monotone gradient against clinical outcome proxy.

Clinically, this framework provides a reproducible methodological pathway for CPET
safety assessment in Chinese elderly hypertensive patients: personalised conditional
quantile reference modelling addresses the systematic failure of Western equations,
the explicit indeterminate zone delivers a transparent methodological acknowledgement for
data-poor samples, and the instability override rules ensure that acute safety
signals are not obscured by reserve capacity scores.

The next step is to deepen analysis at the breath-by-breath waveform level---using
the current stratum assignments as target population screening entry-points, incorporating richer
temporal signals, and conducting prospective follow-up to independently validate
the prognostic value of the safety strata, ultimately enabling the transition from
a methodological framework to a clinical decision support tool.
